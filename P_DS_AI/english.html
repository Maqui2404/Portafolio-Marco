English Grammar Reference

Quantifiers

1. Mucho (Many/Much)

Quantifier Count Nouns Non-Count Nouns Example
Many ✔️ Yes ❌ No Many apples / Many students
Much ❌ No ✔️ Yes Much milk / Much time
A lot of ✔️ Yes ✔️ Yes A lot of chairs / A lot of sugar
Lots of ✔️ Yes ✔️ Yes Lots of cars / Lots of water
Plenty of ✔️ Yes ✔️ Yes Plenty of books / Plenty of money

2. Poco (Few/Little)

Quantifier Count Nouns Non-Count Nouns Example
Few ✔️ Yes ❌ No Few students / Few options
A few ✔️ Yes ❌ No A few books / A few chairs
Little ❌ No ✔️ Yes Little time / Little effort
A little ❌ No ✔️ Yes A little sugar / A little milk

Relative Pronouns

Relative Pronoun Use Explanation Example
Who People (subject) Refers to people as subject of the relative clause The girl who sings is my sister.
Whom People (object) Refers to people as object of the relative clause The teacher whom the students admire is retiring.
Which Objects/animals Refers to things or animals; can also refer to a complete idea The car which I bought is red.
That People/objects (restrictive) Alternative to who, whom or which in restrictive clauses The song that she wrote is beautiful.
Whose Possession Indicates possession for people, animals or things The boy whose dog is barking lives here.
Where Places Refers to a place where something happens This is the house where I grew up.
When Time Refers to a moment or period of time I remember the day when we met.
