Easy Train AI

Plataforma de Entrenamiento IA

Descripción del Proyecto

Easy Train AI es una aplicación web interactiva diseñada para simplificar el entrenamiento de modelos de redes neuronales, utilizando cualquier tipo de datos. Su interfaz intuitiva permite cargar conjuntos de datos en formatos comunes como CSV, TXT y Excel, configurar arquitecturas personalizadas de redes neuronales, entrenar modelos y realizar predicciones en tiempo real. La aplicación está dirigida a estudiantes, profesionales e investigadores, democratizando el acceso al machine learning sin necesidad de conocimientos avanzados en programación.

Objetivos

  • Simplificar el desarrollo y evaluación de redes neuronales.
  • Democratizar el acceso a la inteligencia artificial.
  • Proporcionar una herramienta flexible y escalable.

Chi Researcher

Herramienta Interactiva para Pruebas de Chi-Cuadrado

Descripción del Proyecto

Chi-Researcher es una aplicación web diseñada para realizar análisis estadísticos de Chi-Cuadrado de manera sencilla y accesible. La herramienta permite cargar datos en formatos como CSV o Excel, seleccionar variables categóricas y realizar pruebas estadísticas como independencia, bondad de ajuste y homogeneidad. La aplicación genera gráficos interactivos y permite descargar informes en formato TXT con resultados detallados y sus interpretaciones. Está dirigida a estudiantes, investigadores y profesionales que buscan realizar análisis estadísticos con facilidad y precisión.

Objetivos

How GPUs Kill Threads in Neural Network Training (Springer)

ICDTA 2024 DOI: 10.1007/978-3-031-68660-3_6

Abstract

The optimization and exploration of computing resources continue significant challenges in challenges in the field of neural network training. This paper present an analysis of the effectiveness of several computational architectures, including Graphics Processing Units (GPUs), Central Processing Units (CPUs), and CPUs using threaded techniques, in supporting neural network training. The research highlights the significant differences in training times across different platforms, demonstrating the effectiveness of GPU-based techniques. In order to guarantee a thorough assessment, the research uses two separate datasets: MNIST(Modified National Institute of Standards and Technology), which has 60,000 photos, and USPS(United States Postal Service), which has 7,291 images. These datasets provide the basis of our empirical work, representing a range of dimensions and complexity. The investigation's findings demonstrate the superiority of GPUs in accelerating neural network training, also provide insightful information on how best to use compute resources for these kinds of jobs. This study offers a thorough understanding of the dynamics involved in neural network training by analyzing the minute variations in performance across various computational architectures. Additionally, it lays the groundwork for future investigations into how to make these training procedures more effective, which might result in faster progress in the field of artificial intelligence.

Integration of Text Mining and Complex Social Network Analysis for Quantitative Characterization of Discursive Evolution in Artificial Intelligence Through Streaming Platforms (Scopus Q2)

VISUAL REVIEW 2024 DOI: 10.62161/revvisual.v16.5288

Abstract

This study provides a quantitative characterization of the discursive evolution in Artificial Intelligence (AI) across streaming platforms (such as YouTube), by integrating text mining and complex social network analysis. Using a comprehensive corpus from leading platforms, natural language processing algorithms were employed to analyze the textual content, identifying patterns, emerging themes, and shifts in discourse about AI. Concurrently, a social network analysis was conducted to examine the interaction structures and the influence of different actors in disseminating information. The findings reveal significant trends in the presentation and perception of AI, highlighting the evolution of specific themes, differences in perception among various groups, and the influence of factors such as technological advancements and global events. This analysis provides a deeper understanding of AI communication and perception in the digital realm, offering valuable insights for academics, communicators, and policymakers in the field of Artificial Intelligence.

Keywords
